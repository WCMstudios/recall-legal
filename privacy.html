Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 4, 2026

Recall is a reading-retention app: you bring your books and highlights, and Recall turns them into spaced-repetition recall cards and quick daily reviews. This policy explains what we collect, why, and the choices you have.

This policy applies to the Recall iOS app and this website (together, the "Service"), operated by the maker of Recall ("Recall", "we", "us"). By using the Service you agree to this policy.

Information we collect

Account information

  • Guest accounts. You can start using Recall without signing up. We create an anonymous account tied to your device so your library has somewhere to live. It is not linked to your identity.
  • Sign in with Apple. If you secure your account, we receive the name and email address you choose to share through Apple. If you use Apple's "Hide My Email", we only ever see Apple's private relay address.

Content you create or import

  • Books, highlights, notes, the concept cards generated from them, your review history, reflections, reading sessions, goals, and streaks.
  • Content you import from third parties you choose to connect or upload, including Readwise (via an access token you provide), Kindle "My Clippings" files, and Goodreads CSV exports.

Voice reflections

If you record a spoken reflection, the audio is sent to OpenAI's Whisper service to transcribe it. The transcript is saved as your reflection. We do not retain the audio recording after transcription completes.

Device permissions

  • Microphone is used only while you are recording a voice reflection.
  • Notifications are used to send local reminders (such as the daily recall nudge) from your device. You can turn these off in iOS Settings.

Diagnostic information

We may collect limited technical information needed to operate the Service reliably, such as error reports. Recall does not include third-party advertising or cross-app tracking SDKs.

Waitlist

If you join the launch waitlist on our website, we collect the email address you submit, the web page that referred you (if any), and the time you joined. We use this to let you know when Recall opens for iOS and to send occasional product updates. You can unsubscribe at any time, and you can ask us to delete your address by emailing us (see Contact below). Waitlist entries are stored on our behalf by our database provider, Supabase.

How we use your information

  • To provide the Service: store your library, generate recall cards, transcribe voice reflections, schedule reviews, sync your data across your devices, and send the reminders you enable.
  • To operate, secure, debug, and improve the Service.
  • To respond to your support requests.

AI processing

Two features rely on AI providers acting on our behalf:

  • Concept cards. Your highlights and notes are sent to Anthropic to generate recall cards.
  • Transcription. Voice-reflection audio is sent to OpenAI (Whisper) to produce text.

These providers process your content only to perform these tasks and do not use it to train their models.

Third-party services

We share data only with service providers that help us run Recall, and only as needed:

  • Supabase — authentication, database, file storage, and hosting.
  • Anthropic — generating concept cards from your highlights.
  • OpenAI — transcribing voice reflections with Whisper.
  • Apple — Sign in with Apple, and App Store subscription billing.
  • Readwise — only if you connect it, to import your highlights.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share it for advertising. We may disclose information if required by law or to protect the rights, safety, and security of our users and the Service.

Subscriptions

Recall Pro is sold as an auto-renewing subscription through the Apple App Store. Apple processes your payment; we do not receive or store your payment-card details. Apple shares limited transaction and subscription-status information with us so we can unlock Pro features.

Data retention and deletion

We keep your information for as long as your account is active. You can delete your account at any time from Settings inside the app, which removes your data from our active database. Residual copies may persist in routine encrypted backups for a short period (currently about 7 days) before being overwritten.

Your rights and choices

  • Access, correct, or delete your data (delete via Settings, or contact us).
  • Control microphone and notification access in iOS Settings.
  • Disconnect Readwise at any time.
  • Depending on where you live (for example the EEA, UK, or California), you may have additional rights such as data portability or the right to object to certain processing. Contact us to exercise them.
  • If you are in the EEA, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection authority, including the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens).

Security

Your data is access-controlled so that each account can reach only its own content, and it is encrypted in transit. No method of storage or transmission is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information.

Children

Recall is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your country), and we do not knowingly collect their personal information.

International transfers

We and our service providers may process your information in countries other than your own. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards for these transfers.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date above, and significant changes will be communicated in the app where appropriate.
